1.3 Subtraction Of Whole Numbers

Subtraction

Subtraction is a core arithmetic operation where one quantity is removed from another, effectively "taking away." This process is the inverse of addition, which combines quantities. Visually, subtraction can be represented on a number line by moving left from the starting number (minuend) by the amount being subtracted (subtrahend). The symbol used to denote subtraction is the familiar minus sign (-).

Minus minus (-) is a symbol that is always in the middle, but if you compare the minus and plus, minus is the opposite of plus , minus is deduction.

Parts Of Subtraction

Minuend The number from which another number is subtracted.

SubtrahendThe number that is subtracted from the minuend.

Difference The result of subtraction.

Subtraction With Regrouping

Subtraction Regrouping (also known as "borrowing") is a method used when performing subtraction of numbers, particularly when the top digit in a column is smaller than the bottom digit. It involves borrowing from the next column to make the subtraction possible.

Steps in Subtracting With Regrouping

1. Set up the problem: Write the numbers in a column, with the larger number on top and the smaller number on the bottom.

2.Check each column: Start from the rightmost column. If the digit on top is smaller than the digit below, you'll need to "borrow" from the next column to the left.

3.Borrow from the next column:
- Take 1 from the digit in the next column to the left.
- This makes that digit 1 less, and you add 10 to the current column (the one you're subtracting).

4.Perform the subtraction: Now you can subtract the digits in that column because you’ve made the top digit large enough by borrowing.

5.Repeat for each column: Move to the next column, and repeat the process until you've completed the subtraction.

Example:

Solve 21 - 17 = ?


1. Set up the problem:

Write the numbers vertically, with the larger number (21) on top and the smaller number (17) below it, aligning the ones and tens places.


2. Start with the ones place:

Look at the ones column: 1 - 7. Since 1 is less than 7, we need to regroup.


3. Regroup from the tens place:

Borrow 1 ten from the tens place in 21. This leaves 1 ten in the tens place. The 1 ten we borrowed becomes 10 ones. Add those 10 ones to the 1 one already in the ones place, making it 11 ones.


4. Subtract the ones:

Now we have 11 ones - 7 ones, which equals 4 ones. Write 4 in the one place of the answer.


5. Subtract the tens:

Look at the tens column: 1 ten - 1 ten = 0 tens. Write 0 in the tens place of the answer.


6. The answer:

The answer is 4.
