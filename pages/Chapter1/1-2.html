1.2 Addition Of Whole Numbers

Addition

Addition is a core arithmetic operation where two or more numbers are combined to determine their total value.This process is symbolized by the plus sign (+), which indicates the act of joining quantities. The result of an addition operation is called the sum , representing the overall amount obtained by putting the numbers together. Essentially, addition allows us to quantify the combined value of multiple sets, providing a foundation for more complex mathematical calculations.

Parts Of Addition

Addends are numbers being added together.

Sum is the result of addition.

How Addtion Works?

1. Identify the Addends – You have two numbers to add: 5 and 8. These are the addends.

2. Start with One Number – Begin with one of the numbers, let's say 5.

3. Add the Second Number – Now, add the second number, 8, to the first number. Imagine counting 8 steps forward from 5.

4. Count or Calculate – You can count up from 5: 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, 13. Or, you can recall that 5 + 8 equals 13.

5. State the Sum – The result, 13, is the sum of 5 and 8.

Examples

Addition With Regrouping

Addition With Regrouping is a method used in math when adding numbers together. The numbers are written vertically, lining up the place values of the two numbers.

Steps In Addition With Regroupings


Solve 64 + 38 = ?


Step 1: Align The Numbers


Write the numbers vertically, ensuring that the digits in the ones place, tens place, hundreds place, and so on, are aligned in columns.



Step 2: Add the ones place


First, add the digits in the ones place: 4 (from 64) + 8 (from 38) = 12.


Since 12 is a two-digit number, write down the 2 in the ones place of the sum, and regroup (carry over) the 1 to the tens place.



Step 3: Add the tens place


Now, add the digits in the tens place along with the regrouped 1: 6 (from 64) + 3 (from 38) + 1 (the carried over 1) = 10.


Write down the 0 in the tens place and carry over the 1 to the hundreds place.


Step 4: Final sum


Now you have:

– Tens: 0 (from step 2)
– Hundreds: 1 (carried over) So the final sum is 102.


Therefore, 64 + 38 = 102.

Examples
