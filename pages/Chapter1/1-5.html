1.5 Division Of Whole Numbers

Division

Divisionis a fundamental arithmetic operation, the inverse of multiplication, used to split a quantity into equal parts. It determines how many times one number (the divisor) fits into another (the dividend). Represented by the symbol "÷", division effectively shares an amount into equal-sized groups. This process helps us understand how many times a divisor is contained within a dividend.

Parts Of Divion

Dividend This is the number that is being divided. It's the total amount that you want to split or share.

Divisor This is the number that you are dividing by. It indicates how many groups you are dividing the dividend into.

Quotient This is the answer to the division problem. It represents how many are in each group.

RemainderSometimes, a number cannot be divided evenly. The remainder is the amount left over after the division is completed.

How to Divide?

Solve 10 ÷ 5 = ?


1. Identify the dividend and divisor:

The dividend is the number being divided (in this case, 10).
The divisor is the number you're dividing by (in this case, 5).


2. Determine how many times the divisor fits into the dividend:

Ask yourself, "How many times does 5 go into 10?"


3. Calculate the quotient:

In this case, 5 goes into 10 exactly 2 times. Therefore, 10 ÷ 5 = 2.

Long Division

Long division is a method used to divide large numbers, breaking down the process into smaller, more manageable steps. It's particularly useful when the divisor (the number you're dividing by) has multiple digits. Here's a breakdown:

Steps of Long Division


The process involves these key steps, often remembered by the acronym DMSB:


1. Set up the problem:

Write 53 inside the division bracket.
Write 10 outside the division bracket.


2. Divide:

"How many times does 10 go into 5?" It doesn't.
"How many times does 10 go into 53?" The answer is 5.
Write 5 above the 3 in the dividend.


3. Multiply:

Multiply the quotient (5) by the divisor (10): 5 x 10 = 50.
Write 50 below the 53 in the dividend.


4. Subtract:

Subtract 50 from 53: 53 - 50 = 3.
Write 3 below the 50.


5. Identify the Remainder:

Since there are no more digits in the dividend to bring down, the remaining 3 is the remainder.
Write "R 3" or "remainder 3" next to the quotient.


Remainders in Long Division

What is Remainder?

When there is a number left over, for example 11 divided by 2
11 ÷ 2 = 5 remainder 1.
In this example 1 is the remainder.

Steps of Long Division with Remainder


1. Set up the problem:

Write 53 inside the division bracket.
Write 10 outside the division bracket.

2. Divide:

Determine how many times the divisor goes into the first digit(s) of the dividend.
Write this number (the quotient) above the dividend.


3. Multiply:

Multiply the quotient by the divisor.
Write the result below the corresponding digits of the dividend.


4. Subtract:

Subtract the product from the corresponding digits of the dividend.
Write the difference below the line.


5. Bring Down:

Bring down the next digit of the dividend and write it next to the difference.


6. Result:

The quotient is 5, and the remainder is 3.
Therefore, 53 ÷ 10 = 5 R 3.


Examples


1. 12 ÷ 3 = 4

2. 20 ÷ 5 = 4

3. 36 ÷ 6 = 6

4. 48 ÷ 8 = 6

5. 100 ÷ 10 = 10

6. 13 ÷ 3 = 4 remainder 1

7. 27 ÷ 5 = 5 remainder 2

8. 38 ÷ 7 = 5 remainder 3

9. 50 ÷ 9 = 5 remainder 5

10. 105 ÷ 10 = 10 remainder 5
