3.3 Multiplication and Division of Decimals

Multiplying Decimals

a. Multiplying Decimals by Whole Numbers:

When multiplying a decimal by a whole number, it's very similar to multiplying whole numbers. The key difference lies in placing the decimal point in the final product.


Step 1: Multiply the numbers as if they were both whole numbers, ignoring the decimal point for now.


Step 2: Count the total number of decimal places in the decimal factor.


Step 3: Place the decimal point in the product so that it has the same number of decimal places as the decimal factor.


Example 1: Multiply 3.25 by 4.


325 x 4 = 1300 * 3.25 has two decimal places. Therefore, 3.25 x 4 = 13.00, which is simply 13.


b. Multiplying Decimals by Other Decimals:

The process is similar when multiplying two decimal numbers.


Step 1: Multiply the numbers as if they were both whole numbers.


Step 2: Count the total number of decimal places in both factors.


Step 3: Place the decimal point in the product so that it has the same total number of decimal places as the combined decimal places of the factors.


Example 2: Multiply 2.5 by 1.2.


25 x 12 = 300 2.5 has one decimal place, and 1.2 has one decimal place, totaling two decimal places. Therefore, 2.5 x 1.2 = 3.00, which is simply 3.

Dividing Decimals

a. Dividing Decimals by Whole Numbers:

Dividing a decimal by a whole number is similar to dividing whole numbers. The decimal point in the quotient is placed directly above the decimal point in the dividend.


Step 1: Divide as you would with whole numbers.
Step 2: Place the decimal point in the quotient directly above the decimal point in the dividend.


Example 3: Divide 8.4 by 2.


8 ÷ 2 = 4 * 4 ÷ 2 = 2 Therefore, 8.4 ÷ 2 = 4.2.


b. Dividing Decimals by Other Decimals:


To divide a decimal by another decimal, we need to make the divisor a whole number.


Step 1: Move the decimal point in the divisor to the right until it becomes a whole number.
Step 2: Move the decimal point in the dividend the same number of places to the right. Add zeros if necessary.
Step 3: Divide as you would with a whole number divisor.


Example 4: Divide 7.5 by 2.5.


Move the decimal point one place to the right in 2.5, making it 25. * Move the decimal point one place to the right in 7.5, making it 75. * Now, divide 75 by 25. * 75 ÷ 25 = 3 * Therefore, 7.5 ÷ 2.5 = 3.
