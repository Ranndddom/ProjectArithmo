3.2 Addition and Subtraction of Decimals


Decimalsare an essential part of our everyday lives. We use them when dealing with money, measurements, and many other situations. Adding and subtracting decimals is a fundamental skill that builds upon our understanding of whole number operations. This lesson will guide you through the process, emphasizing the importance of aligning decimal points and handling regrouping.

Steps in Addition And Subtraction Of Decimals

1. Aligning Decimal Points

The most crucial step in adding or subtracting decimals is to align the decimal points vertically. This ensures that you are adding or subtracting digits with the same place value (tenths with tenths, hundredths with hundredths, and so on).

Problem: 1.76 + 3.48

Solution:


6 + 8 = 14. Write down 4, carry over 1.
1 (carried) + 7 + 4 = 12. Write down 2, carry over 1.
1 (carried) + 1 + 3 = 5. Write down 5.


3. Subtracting Decimals

Similar to addition, aligning decimal points is the first step in subtraction. Then, subtract the numbers as you would with whole numbers, starting from the rightmost digit. Remember to borrow (regroup) when a digit in the top number is smaller than the corresponding digit in the bottom number.

The tenths place is now 1. 1-8: We need to borrow 1 from the ones place. 11-8=3.
