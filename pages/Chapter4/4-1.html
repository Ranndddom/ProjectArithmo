4.1 Introduction to Integers

Understanding Integers and The Number Line

Integers are whole numbers that can be positive, negative, or zero. They do not include fractions or decimals.

To learn what integers are we need to start with thewhole numbers

Whole numbers are the starting thing with zero on the number line.

Number Line
- A visual representation of numbers on a straight line.
- Numbers are placed at equal intervals along the line.
- Extends infinitely in both directions (positive and negative).

ZeroThis is the integer right in the middle of the number line. It is neither positive nor negative.

Negative integersThese are the numbers to the left of zero on the number line (-1, -2, -3, -4, 5,...).

Positive integersThese are the numbers to the right of zero on the number line (1, 2, 3, 4, 5,...). They are sometimes called “natural numbers” or “counting numbers”

Comparing And Ordering Integers

Now that we know what integers let us furthermore discuss how to compare and order integers.


In comparing integers we need to understand that the numbers to the right of zero gets bigger and bigger.


While the numbers to the left of zero get smaller and smaller. Therefore Zero is greater than negative numbers but less than positive numbers.

Rules For Comparing

Positive vs. Negative: Any positive integer is greater than any negative integer.

Zero: Zero is greater than any negative integer and less than any positive integer.

Comparing Positives: The larger the positive number, the greater its value.

Comparing Negatives: The larger the negative number (ignoring the minus sign), the smaller its value>

less than symbol (<) indicates that the number on the left is smaller than the number on the right. For example, 2 < 6 means 2 is less than 6.

greater than symbol (>) indicates that the number on the left is larger than the number on the right. For example, 10 > 3 means 10 is greater than 3.

Examples


Let's compare 0 to -5

Since since -5 is negative integer and it can be found to the left of number zero this means that zero is greater than negative five


Answer:0 > -5


Let's compare 0 to 5

Since five here is positive and and it can be bound to the right of zero this means that zero is less than positive five


Answer:0 < 5
