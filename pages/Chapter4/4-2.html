4.2 Addition and Subtraction of Integers

Addition Of Integers

In this lesson we are going to be working on adding and subtracting integers.


Will start by adding integers.


Adding integers involves combining their values. Here's how it works!


Same Signs: If the integers have the same sign (both positive or both negative), add their absolute values and keep the original sign.

Different Signs: If the integers have different signs (one positive and one negative), subtract their absolute values. The result will have the sign of the integer with the larger absolute value.

Positive + Positive:


12 + 5 = 17
2 + 8 = 10


Negative + Negative:


(-6) + (-3) = -9
(-1) + (-11) = -12


Positive + Negative:


9 + (-2) = 7
(-5) + 1 = -4
15 + (-15) = 0


Any integer plus zero equals itself.


7 + 0 = 7
(-4) + 0 = -4


Subtraction Of Integers

Subtracting integers can seem tricky at first, but it becomes easier with a little practice. Here's a breakdown of the rules and some examples to help you understand:

The Golden Rule of Integer Subtraction


The key to subtracting integers is to change the subtraction problem into an additional problem. Here's how:


Keep the first number the same.

Change the subtraction sign (-) to an additional sign (+).


Change the sign of the second number to its opposite. (If it's positive, make it negative. If it's negative, make it positive.)


Now, follow the rules for adding integers.


Same Signs: If the integers have the same sign (both positive or both negative), add their absolute values and keep the original sign.

Different Signs: If the integers have different signs (one positive and one negative), subtract their absolute values. The result will have the sign of the integer with the larger absolute value.

Examples of Subtracting Integers

5 - 9


Change to addition: 5 + (-9)
Different signs: 9 - 5 = 4. Since 9 is larger and negative, the answer is -4.
Answer: 5 - 9 = -4


-3 - (-6)


Change to addition: -3 + 6
Different signs: 6 - 3 = 3. Since 6 is larger and positive, the answer is 3.
Answer: -3 - (-6) = 3


-2 - 4


Change to addition: -2 + (-4)
Same signs: 2 + 4 = 6. Keep the negative sign.
Answer: -2 - 4 = -6
