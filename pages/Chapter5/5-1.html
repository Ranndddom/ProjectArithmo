5.1 Order Of Operation

Order Of Operation

The Order Of Operations are the rules that tell us the sequence in which we should solve an expression with multiple operations. The order is called PEMDAS

What Is PEMDAS?


PEMDASprovides a standardized set of rules for evaluating mathematical expressions.

PEMDAS Stands For:

P - Parenthesis
E - Exponents
M - Multiplication
D - Division
A - Addition
S - Subtraction

By following PEMDAS steps, you can simplify and accurately solve mathematical expressions, ensuring a correct final answer.


P: Solve the calculation or equation which is present in the parenthesis or brackets like small brackets( ), curly brackets{ } or big brackets[ ]. Priority is given to brackets first.

E: Exponential expressions should be calculated first before the operations of multiplication, division. Addition, and subtraction. Usually, they are expressed in power roots.

M and D: Then perform multiplication or division from left to right, whichever comes first in the equation.

A and S: At last, perform addition or subtraction whichever comes first while moving from left to right.

What is an Example Of a PEMDAS Math Problem?

Example


Solve 6 + (9 x 7) =

Answer: 6 + (9 x 7) = 69

Explanation:First, we see parenthesis comes first in the PEMDAS acronym. So we would multiply 9 x 7 because it is in the parenthesis. Therefore, 9 x 7 = 63. Then we would add 6 and 63 to get the 69 as our answer.

The order of operations in mathematics helps determine the right value for an equation. Let us see how to solve mathematical equations using PEMDAS rules.


Solve 45 ÷ (5 × 7) + 4 = ?


As per the PEMDAS rule, first, we have to perform the operation which is in the parentheses


45 ÷ 35 + 4


The division should be performed.


So, 45 ÷ 35 = 1.2


And the last, addition.


So, 1.2 + 4 = 5.2


Therefore, 45 ÷ ( 5 × 7) + 4 = 5.2
