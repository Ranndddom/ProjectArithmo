2.2 Adition and Subraction of Fractions

Finding common denominators

common denominator is a common multiple of the denominators of two or more fractions. It's used to add, subtract, or compare fractions with different denominators.

Here are the steps to find a common denominator:

1. List the denominators of the fractions.
2. Find the least common multiple (LCM) of the denominators.
3. Use the LCM as the common denominator.


Example


Find the common denominator of 1/4 and 1/6.


1. List the denominators: 4 and 6.
2. Find the LCM of 4 and 6: 12.
3. Use 12 as the common denominator: 1/4 = 3/12 and 1/6 = 2/12.

Addition Of Fraction

Adding Fractions with Like Denominators

When adding fractions with like denominators, you simply add the numerators and keep the same denominator.

Example

1. 1/4 + 1/4 = 2/4
2. 3/8 + 2/8 = 5/8


Adding Fractions with Unlike Denominators

When adding fractions with unlike denominators, you need to find the least common multiple (LCM) of the denominators.

Example

Solve 1/4 + 1/6 = ?


Find the LCM of 4 and 6: 12


Convert both fractions to have a denominator of 12:


1/4 = 3/12
1/6 = 2/12


Now add: 3/12 + 2/12 = 5/12

Subtraction Of Fraction


Subtracting Fractions with Like Denominators

When subtracting fractions with denominators, you simply subtract the numerators and keep the same denominator.


Example:

1. 3/8 - 2/8 = 1/8
2. 5/12 - 3/12 = 2/12


Subtracting Fractions with Unlike Denominators

When subtracting fractions with unlike denominators, you need to find the LCM of the denominators.


Example

Solve 3/4 - 1/6 = ?


Find the LCM of 4 and 6: 12


Convert both fractions to have a denominator of 12:


3/4 = 9/12
1/6 = 2/12


Now subtract: 9/12 - 2/12 = 7/12

How to convert between mixed numbers and improper fractions.

Mixed Number to Improper Fraction

To convert a mixed number to an improper fraction, follow these steps:


1. Multiply the whole number part by the denominator.
2. Add the numerator to the product.
3. Write the result as an improper fraction.


Example


Convert 2 3/4 to an improper fraction.


Multiply whole number part by denominator: 2 × 4 = 8
Add numerator: 8 + 3 = 11
Write as improper fraction: 11/4


Improper Fraction to Mixed Number

To convert an improper fraction to a mixed number, follow these steps:


1. Divide the numerator by the denominator.
2. Write the quotient as the whole number part.
3. Write the remainder as the numerator of the fraction part.


Example:

Convert 11/4 to a mixed number.


Divide numerator by denominator: 11 ÷ 4 = 2 with a remainder of 3
Write quotient as whole number part: 2
Write remainder as numerator: 3
Write as mixed number: 2 3/4
