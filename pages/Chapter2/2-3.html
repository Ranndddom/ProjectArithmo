2.3 Multiplication and Division of fraction

Multiplication Of Fraction

Multiplication of fractions is a process that involves finding the product of two or more fractions. Here's a clear definition:

Multiplication of fractions is an arithmetic operation where the numerators (top numbers) of the fractions are multiplied together to find the numerator of the product, and the denominators (bottom numbers) of the fractions are multiplied together to find the denominator of the product.

Steps In Multiplication Of Fraction

1. Multiply the Numerators:

Multiply the numerators of the two fractions together.
Write the result as the numerator of the product (the answer).


2. Multiply the Denominators:

Multiply the denominators of the two fractions together.
Write the result as the denominator of the product.


3. Simplify (if possible):

Check if the resulting fraction can be simplified.
Find the greatest common factor (GCF) of the numerator and denominator.
Divide both the numerator and denominator by the GCF to get the simplest form of the fraction.

Examples


Solve 1/2 x 2/3


1. Multiply Numerators: 1 x 2 = 2
2. Multiply Denominators: 2 x 3 = 6
3. Result: 2/6
4. Simplify: The GCF of 2 and 6 is 2.

2 ÷ 2 = 1
6 ÷ 2 = 3

Simplified result: 1/3


Solve 1 1/2 x 2 1/4


1 and 1/2 = (1 x 2 + 1) / 2 = 3/2
2 and 1/4 = (2 x 4 + 1) / 4 = 9/4

2. Multiply Numerators: 3 x 9 = 27
3. Multiply Denominators: 2 x 4 = 8
4. Result: 27/8
5. Convert back to a mixed number:** 27 divided by 8 is 3 with a remainder of 3. Therefore the answer is 3 3/8.

Division Of Fraction

Division of fractionsis an arithmetic operation where you multiply the first fraction (the dividend) by the reciprocal of the second fraction (the divisor). The result is the quotient.

Steps In Division Of Fraction

1. Understand the Concept:

Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal.
The reciprocal of a fraction is found by flipping the numerator and denominator.


2. Find the Reciprocal of the Divisor:

The divisor is the fraction you're dividing by.
Flip the numerator and denominator of the divisor to find its reciprocal.
Example: The reciprocal of 2/3 is 3/2.


3. Change Division to Multiplication:

Rewrite the division problem as a multiplication problem.
Keep the first fraction (the dividend) the same.
Change the division symbol (÷) to a multiplication symbol (x).
Replace the divisor with its reciprocal.


4. Multiply the Fractions:

Multiply the numerators of the two fractions together.
Multiply the denominators of the two fractions together.


5. Simplify (if possible):

Check if the resulting fraction can be simplified.
Find the greatest common factor (GCF) of the numerator and denominator.
Divide both the numerator and denominator by the GCF to get the simplest form of the fraction.
If the result is an improper fraction, then convert it to a mixed number.

Examples


Solve 1/2 ÷ 3/4


1. Find the Reciprocal: The reciprocal of 3/4 is 4/3.
2. Change to Multiplication: 1/2 x 4/3
3. Multiply:

1 x 4 = 4
2 x 3 = 6

4. Result: 4/6
5. Simplify: The GCF of 4 and 6 is 2.

4 ÷ 2 = 2
6 ÷ 2 = 3

Simplified result: 2/3


Solve 2 1/3 ÷ 1 1/2


1. Convert to Improper Fractions:

2 1/3 = (2 x 3 + 1) / 3 = 7/3
1 1/2 = (1 x 2 + 1) / 2 = 3/2

2. Find the Reciprocal: The reciprocal of 3/2 is 2/3
3. Change to Multiplication: 7/3 x 2/3
4. Multiply:

7 x 2 = 14
3 x 3 = 9

5. Result: 14/9
6. Convert to Mixed Number:** 14 divided by 9 is 1 with a remainder of 5. Result: 1 5/9
